# Before/After Examples

## Example 1: Throat-clearing opener

**Before:**
> It's worth noting that in today's fast-paced digital landscape, companies are increasingly realizing the fundamental importance of truly understanding their customers' needs. This is why so many organizations are now investing deeply in customer research.

**After:**
> Companies that research customers outperform those that don't. The gap widens every year.

---

## Example 2: False agency + passive voice

**Before:**
> The data suggests that improvements can be made to the onboarding process. Feedback has been received indicating that new users often feel overwhelmed by the initial setup experience.

**After:**
> New users told us setup feels overwhelming. Three steps in the onboarding flow cause most of the drop-off.

---

## Example 3: Binary contrast + narrator-from-a-distance

**Before:**
> This isn't about writing better documentation. It's about building trust. People who feel confused by a product don't complain — they leave. Nobody designed this outcome, but it happens constantly.

**After:**
> Confusing docs cost you customers before they contact support. Most leave without saying why.

---

## Example 4: Rhetorical setup + rule-of-three

**Before:**

**After:**
> Your team needs clear ownership and consistent standards. Everything else follows.
