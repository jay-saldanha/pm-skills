# Formulaic Structures to Break

## Binary contrasts
Pattern: "Not X — it's Y." / "This isn't about X. It's about Y."
Fix: State Y directly. Drop the negation setup.

Bad:  "This isn't about efficiency. It's about trust."
Good: "Customers care about trust."

## Negative listings
Pattern: Listing what something is NOT before saying what it IS.
Fix: State what it is. Skip the disclaimers.

Bad:  "This isn't a bug fix. It isn't a workaround. It's a fundamental redesign."
Good: "The team redesigned the system from scratch."

## Dramatic fragmentation
Pattern: Short. Punchy. Fragments. For. Effect.
Fix: Write complete sentences. Let the content carry weight.

## Rhetorical setup questions
Pattern: "So what does this mean for you?" / "Why does this matter?"
Fix: Just say what it means. The reader will figure out relevance.

## False agency (inanimate subjects doing human things)
Pattern: "The data reveals..." / "The process enables..." / "The framework allows..."
Fix: Name the person. "Engineers use the framework to..."

## The rule-of-three trap
Pattern: "X, Y, and Z." — three items in every list.
Fix: Two items are usually enough. If you need three, make sure the third adds something.

## Metronomic rhythm
Pattern: Every sentence is the same length. Every paragraph ends with a short punchy line. Every transition is "This means that."
Fix: Vary deliberately. Long sentence, short one. Statement, then implication.

## Narrator-from-a-distance
Pattern: "Nobody designed this to work this way." / "People often wonder..." / "Many organizations struggle with..."
Fix: Put the reader in the scene. Use "you" or name a specific person/team.

## Meta-joiners
Pattern: "The rest of this article will cover..." / "In the next section, we'll explore..."
Fix: Delete. Move to the next section.

## Passive escalation
Pattern: "Mistakes were made." / "The decision was taken." / "It has been observed that..."
Fix: Name who did it. "The team made the wrong call." "Legal blocked the release."

## The false pivot
Pattern: "While X is true, Y is also important." (used to introduce every paragraph)
Fix: Pick a structure and commit. Drop the "while/although/however" hedging.
